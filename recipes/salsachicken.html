Crockpot Salsa Chicken Recipe

This recipe is easy, relatively healthy, and super low-maintenance! It can also be simplified, or given more complexity depending on how much time and effort you have to spare!

The basis of this dish is frozen chicken breats with jarred salsa (yes, you heard that right) minimal spices and a can of black beans thrown into the crock pot. Turn the heat on high for 4-6 hours until the chicken falls apart when you pick it up with a fork. You can jazz it up by adding diced onion, bell peppers, jalapenos, or cilantro! The choice is yours.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 large frozen or thawed chicken breasts
  • 1-2 jars of mild-medium salsa
  • 1 can of black beans, drained
  • *Optional*
  • 2 cans of green chiles
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced finely
  • 1 jalapeno, diced finely
  • half of a white or red onion, diced finely
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Take out your crockpot, set the dial to "high"
  2. Add a layer of salsa to cooat the bottom of your pot
  3. Add your chicken breasts, and add the rest of the salsa in to coat the tops and sides
  4. Open and drain your can of black beans, add those in on top
  5. If you are adding the extra optional ingredients, dice those finely and add them to the top too
  6. Leave on high for 4-6 hours, depending on if your chicken was frozen or thawed, Check periodocally to see when the chicken is ready.
  7. Once the chicken is falling apart, shred the chicken and mix everyhting together thoroughly. Salt and pepper to taste, plus any additional spices you want.
